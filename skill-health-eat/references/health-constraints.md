# Health Constraints / 健康限制

Use these as practical guardrails. If a user has prescribed medical targets, follow those instead and encourage clinician/dietitian confirmation.

## Life Stage and Role / 男女老少与特殊阶段

- Adult men with high activity often need larger portions of staple carbs/protein than sedentary adults; avoid assuming "more meat" is the answer.
- Adult women may need attention to iron, calcium, protein, and energy sufficiency, especially with menstruation, pregnancy, lactation, or heavy training.
- Children need regular energy, protein, calcium, iron, fruits/vegetables, and choking-safe textures. Do not recommend aggressive restriction or adult weight-loss framing.
- Older adults often need protein at each meal, fiber, vitamin D/calcium/potassium-rich foods unless restricted, hydration, easy chewing, and lower sodium/saturated fat.
- Pregnant users need food safety: avoid unpasteurized dairy/juice, high-mercury fish, raw/undercooked seafood/eggs/meat, and excess caffeine; ask about gestational diabetes.

## Diabetes / 糖尿病

- Confirm medication and hypoglycemia risk before suggesting skipped meals or fasting.
- Prefer regular balanced meals, nonstarchy vegetables, lean protein, fiber-rich carbs, and unsweetened drinks.
- Use plate method unless the user has a prescribed carb target: half nonstarchy vegetables, quarter lean protein, quarter carbohydrate food.
- Pair carbs with protein/fat/fiber. Avoid sugary drinks and large refined-carb-only meals.
- If symptoms suggest severe hypo/hyperglycemia, prioritize medical guidance and fast-acting carbohydrate only when hypoglycemia is plausible and the user can safely follow their care plan.

## Hypertension / 高血压

- Emphasize lower sodium, more vegetables/fruit/legumes/whole grains, lean protein, unsweetened drinks, and fewer processed foods.
- Avoid salty soups, pickles, cured meats, instant noodles seasoning packets, heavy sauces, and large restaurant portions.
- If kidney disease or potassium restriction is present, do not broadly recommend high-potassium foods.

## High Cholesterol / 高血脂

- Favor unsaturated fats, nuts/seeds if tolerated, fish, legumes, soy foods if tolerated, whole grains, vegetables, fruit, and lean poultry.
- Limit saturated fat-heavy meals: fatty meat, butter/cream, fried foods, pastries, processed meats.
- Avoid framing cholesterol as only "do not eat eggs"; prioritize overall pattern and fiber.

## Gout or High Uric Acid / 痛风或高尿酸

- During a flare, avoid alcohol, organ meats, large portions of red meat, high-purine seafood, and sugary drinks.
- Encourage water, low-fat dairy if tolerated, eggs/tofu/legumes in moderate individualized amounts, vegetables, and whole grains.
- Ask about kidney disease and clinician targets.

## Kidney, Liver, Heart Failure / 肾病、肝病、心衰

- Ask stage/severity and prescribed limits before specific advice.
- Kidney disease may require individualized protein, sodium, potassium, phosphorus, and fluid limits.
- Heart failure may require sodium and fluid limits; avoid recommending large soups, broths, or salty meals.
- Liver disease varies widely; avoid alcohol and ask about ascites, encephalopathy, protein instructions, and clinician plan.

## GI Conditions / 胃肠问题

- For acute nausea/diarrhea, suggest small bland meals, oral hydration, and medical care for severe dehydration, blood, fever, or persistent symptoms.
- For reflux, avoid very large meals, late heavy meals, high-fat/fried foods, alcohol, and trigger foods.
- For constipation, increase fluids and fiber gradually unless there is obstruction risk or medical restriction.

## Allergies and Avoidances / 过敏与忌口

- Treat allergy as strict avoidance. Do not suggest "a little" exposure.
- Screen at least the Big 9: milk, eggs, fish, crustacean shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, sesame.
- Consider hidden ingredients: sauces, broths, bakery items, dressings, protein bars, plant milks, spice mixes.
- For severe allergies, include cross-contact caution for restaurants/fryers/shared utensils.
- For dislikes/picky eating, offer nutritionally similar substitutions without arguing taste.

## Sleep Deprivation / 熬夜

- Prioritize hydration, regular balanced meals, moderate caffeine timing, protein, fiber-rich carbs, fruit/vegetables, and not overloading with sugar/fried foods.
- If close to sleep, choose lighter warm meals and avoid heavy greasy/spicy meals and excess caffeine.
- If user skipped meals while awake long hours, avoid a giant refined-carb meal; split into a balanced meal plus planned snack.

## Heavy Physical Labor or Intense Exercise / 重体力劳动或高强度运动

- Replenish fluids, sodium only if heavy sweating and no sodium restriction, carbohydrate for glycogen, and protein for repair.
- Include easy carbs plus protein within the next meal: rice/noodles/potatoes/oats/bread plus eggs/fish/chicken/tofu/yogurt if tolerated.
- Ask about heat exposure, dizziness, cramps, and medical restrictions.

## Prolonged Cognitive Work / 长时间高强度用脑

- Prioritize steady energy: balanced carbs, protein, healthy fats, hydration, and micronutrient-dense foods.
- Avoid only sweets/caffeine. Suggest fruit + yogurt/nuts, egg/tofu + grain, soup plus protein, or sandwich/salad bowl.
- If the user worked 20+ hours, consider sleep timing and digestion: lighter meal before sleep, larger balanced meal after waking if needed.
