# Meal Framework / 餐食方案框架

## Base Plate / 基础餐盘

Use this as the default for most adults:

- 1/2 nonstarchy vegetables: leafy greens, broccoli, cucumber, tomato, peppers, mushrooms, zucchini, eggplant, cabbage.
- 1/4 protein: fish, poultry, lean meat, eggs, tofu/tempeh, beans/lentils, yogurt/cottage cheese if tolerated.
- 1/4 carbohydrate: rice, noodles, oats, potatoes, corn, whole-grain bread, pasta, fruit, beans.
- Add modest unsaturated fat: olive/canola oil, avocado, nuts/seeds if tolerated.
- Drink: water, unsweetened tea/coffee, milk/soy milk if suitable.

Adjust portions for child/teen growth, adult sex/body size, pregnancy/lactation, older adult appetite, heavy labor, weight goals, and medical restrictions.

## Time of Day / 按时间调整

- Morning: protein + fiber-rich carb + fruit/vegetable. Avoid sweet drink + pastry only.
- Midday: full balanced meal; best time for larger vegetables/staple/protein if busy later.
- Evening: balanced but not excessively greasy/spicy/heavy, especially close to sleep.
- Late night after work: small warm balanced meal if hungry; avoid huge meals before bed. Examples: vegetable egg noodle soup, congee plus egg/tofu, Greek yogurt + oats + fruit, turkey/egg sandwich.
- Skipped breakfast: lunch should not be all refined carbs; include protein and vegetables.
- Skipped lunch: afternoon snack may prevent overeating dinner: yogurt/soy milk + fruit, egg + whole-grain toast, tofu pudding without syrup, nuts if tolerated.
- Skipped dinner intentionally: check medication/hypoglycemia risk; offer light protein/fiber snack if needed.

## Chinese + Western Options / 中西结合

Chinese-style:

- Brown/white rice mix + steamed fish/chicken/tofu + two vegetables.
- Tomato egg noodles with extra greens and less oil/salt.
- Congee with egg/tofu/chicken plus side vegetables; better for late night or GI discomfort.
- Stir-fried vegetables + lean protein + small rice portion; request less oil and less salt.
- Hotpot: clear broth, vegetables, tofu, lean meat, moderate staple; avoid heavy dipping sauce and processed meat.

Western-style:

- Oatmeal + Greek yogurt/milk/soy milk + berries/banana + nuts/seeds if tolerated.
- Salad/grain bowl: greens + chicken/fish/tofu/beans + quinoa/rice/potato + olive-oil vinaigrette.
- Whole-grain sandwich/wrap with lean protein + vegetables + fruit.
- Pasta with tomato sauce, vegetables, and lean protein; avoid cream-heavy sauces for cholesterol concerns.
- Soup + protein side + bread/potato, with sodium caution.

Fusion:

- Rice bowl with tofu/chicken, stir-fried vegetables, avocado/olive-oil dressing.
- Whole-grain toast + egg/tofu scramble + cucumber/tomato.
- Soba or whole-wheat noodles with chicken/tofu, greens, sesame-free sauce if sesame allergy.

## Budget and Environment / 预算与环境

Always include feasible substitutions:

- Low budget: eggs, tofu, beans/lentils, seasonal vegetables, frozen vegetables, oats, potatoes, rice, canned fish/beans with low sodium if possible.
- Mid budget: chicken breast/thigh without skin, fish, yogurt, fresh fruit, mixed grains, olive/canola oil.
- Higher budget: seafood, lean beef in modest portions, specialty grains, pre-cut vegetables, prepared healthy bowls.
- Convenience store: unsweetened milk/soy milk/yogurt, boiled eggs, banana/apple, plain nuts, salad, tuna/chicken pack, whole-grain bread, oatmeal; watch sodium/sugar.
- Restaurant/delivery: ask for less oil/salt/sauce on side; choose grilled/steamed/boiled over fried; split large portions; add vegetables.
- Dorm/office: microwave oats, instant rice + tuna/tofu + salad, yogurt + fruit, boiled eggs, low-sodium soup if available.

## Option Template / 推荐模板

For each option:

Chinese:
"方案 A：名称。怎么点/怎么做：... 份量：... 为什么适合你：... 替换：如果预算/过敏/不爱吃/买不到，可以换成..."

English:
"Option A: Name. How to order/make it: ... Portion: ... Why it fits: ... Swaps: for budget/allergy/dislike/unavailable foods, use..."

## Practical Portion Language / 份量表达

Use household estimates:

- Protein: palm-sized cooked fish/chicken/lean meat; 2 eggs; 1/2-1 block tofu; 3/4-1 cup beans.
- Carbs: 1 fist rice/noodles/potato/oats; reduce or spread out for diabetes if needed.
- Vegetables: 2 fists or half the plate.
- Fat: 1 thumb oil/nut butter, or a small handful nuts if tolerated.

Avoid exact calories/macros unless the user gives height, weight, goal, activity, medical details, and asks for it.
