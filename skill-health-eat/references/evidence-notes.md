# Evidence Notes / 证据与更新

Use current authoritative sources when disease-specific claims, numeric limits, pregnancy/child guidance, or medication interactions matter. Cite sources in user-facing answers when browsing was used.

## Baseline Sources Checked During Skill Creation

- USDA Food and Nutrition Service says the Dietary Guidelines for Americans are the cornerstone for federal nutrition programs and provide food-based recommendations to promote health, prevent diet-related chronic disease, and meet nutrient needs. The current edition listed on the USDA page is Dietary Guidelines for Americans, 2025-2030. Source: https://www.fns.usda.gov/cnpp/dietary-guidelines-americans
- CDC diabetes meal planning guidance emphasizes individualized plans, nonstarchy vegetables, fewer added sugars/refined grains, whole foods, carb counting, and the plate method. Source: https://www.cdc.gov/diabetes/healthy-eating/diabetes-meal-planning.html
- FDA food allergy guidance identifies the U.S. major allergens as milk, eggs, fish, crustacean shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, and sesame, and stresses strict avoidance plus medical evaluation. Source: https://www.fda.gov/food/buy-store-serve-safe-food/food-allergies-what-you-need-know
- FDA older-adult label guidance notes older adults often need more fiber, vitamin D, calcium, and potassium, and less saturated fat, sodium, and added sugars, while individual needs vary. Source: https://www.fda.gov/food/nutrition-facts-label/using-nutrition-facts-label-older-adults
- American Heart Association diet/lifestyle recommendations emphasize vegetables, fruits, whole grains, healthy proteins, minimally processed foods, unsaturated oils, less added sugar, less salt, and practical application wherever food is prepared or consumed. Source: https://www.heart.org/en/healthy-living/healthy-eating/eat-smart/nutrition-basics/aha-diet-and-lifestyle-recommendations

## Browse When / 什么时候联网确认

- The user asks for "latest", "current", "official", "evidence-based", or cites a specific guideline.
- The answer includes numeric medical targets: sodium, potassium, carbohydrates, protein for kidney disease, cholesterol/saturated fat, pregnancy caffeine, child nutrition, sports hydration.
- The user has multiple conditions with conflicting constraints, such as diabetes + kidney disease, hypertension + potassium restriction, gout + kidney disease, pregnancy + diabetes.
- The user asks about a medication, supplement, commercial diet, new product, restaurant menu, or local availability.

## Citation Style / 引用方式

Keep citations short:

Chinese:
"依据 CDC 糖尿病餐盘法，先用半盘非淀粉蔬菜、四分之一蛋白、四分之一主食做基础，再按你的血糖/用药调整。"

English:
"Using the CDC plate-method framing, start with half nonstarchy vegetables, one quarter protein, and one quarter carbohydrate, then adjust around your glucose targets and medication plan."
